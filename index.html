02 Mid-Application: Ad-Hoc Requests & Back-Office Follow-Up

During a home loan application, the bank's credit assessor may identify gaps or need verification on specific items. How this is communicated back to the customer — and how they respond — varies significantly across the Big 4. This section covers the customer experience of receiving and acting on ad-hoc requests: additional documents, clarification questions, property conditions, account nomination, contract upload, and more.
Common Triggers for Mid-Application Requests
📄 Additional payslip / income proof 🏠 Property valuation shortfall 💳 Nominate repayment account 📋 Contract of Sale upload ✍️ Clarify credit history item 🏗️ Builder's insurance / council plans 🧾 Lease agreement (rental income) 👥 Co-borrower identity verification

CommBank — Ad-Hoc Request Flow

Step 1
🔔 Push Notification
Customer receives push, SMS, or email: "Action needed on your home loan application." No inline context on reason yet.
Push + SMS + Email
Step 2
📍 App Tracker — Action Banner
Inside App Tracker, a red/orange banner shows the outstanding item — e.g. "Upload pay slips" or "Upload contract of sale." Status shown as blocked.
Visual Alert
Step 3
📤 In-App Document Upload
Customer uploads directly via Buy a Home. Camera, files, or photo supported. Per-document status updates to "Received" immediately on upload.
Best-in-class
Step 4
✅ Status Confirmed
Lender reviews upload. Status returns to "In Progress." If further items needed, new action appears. Push notification sent when confirmed.
Real-time

✅ CommBank Ad-Hoc Strengths

  • Push + SMS + email trio means customers rarely miss requests

02 Mid-Application: Ad-Hoc Requests & Back-Office Follow-Up

ANZ Plus is radically different here: because income, identity, and credit data are fully automated, ad-hoc mid-application requests are extremely rare by design. The system auto-verifies most cases — and when something does require follow-up, the ANZ Plus Coaches handle it via in-app chat or call, without requiring document uploads. If additional information is needed, the app prompts the customer inline with clear instructions.
When ANZ Plus Still Needs More Info
🤖 Edge case income not auto-parsed 💳 Nominate ANZ Plus Everyday account for repayments ✍️ eSign loan documents in-app 📹 Coach video call for complex query 👥 Joint applicant — partner completes on their own phone

ANZ Plus — Ad-Hoc Request Flow

Step 1
🔔 In-App Notification
ANZ Plus sends an in-app push notification: "Action needed — tap to see details." No email/SMS by default; app-first design.
App-native
Step 2
📲 Inline Prompt with Context
The application screen shows the specific item needed, with a plain-language explanation and a "Get Help" button linking to a Coach if needed.
Best context clarity
Step 3
💬 Coach Resolution
For most cases, the ANZ Plus Coach can resolve the item in-app via chat, phone, or video call — without requiring a document upload from the customer.
Human-assisted
Step 4
✅ Auto-Progressed
Once condition satisfied, the workflow engine (Temporal) automatically progresses to the next step. Customer sees loan docs ready to sign in seconds.
Temporal engine

02 Mid-Application: Ad-Hoc Requests & Back-Office Follow-Up

Westpac's ad-hoc experience is split across channels: the digital application portal handles document uploads and co-borrower workflows, while follow-up for outstanding conditions is primarily via phone and email from the assigned loan specialist — not proactively surfaced in the Westpac App. Customers with a Westpac account can nominate it for repayments online; non-Westpac accounts require a printed Direct Debit form.
Common Westpac Mid-Application Triggers
📄 Additional income documents 🏠 Valuation shortfall — bank negotiation 💳 Nominate repayment account (Westpac or other) 📋 Contract of Sale / Plans upload via portal 👥 Third party nominated to provide documents 🧾 Co-borrower separately signs into digital application

Westpac — Ad-Hoc Request Flow

Phone + Email only

Loan pack issued digitally via Westpac Live (online banking). eSign available — praised by brokers for speed. Physical doc fallback for edge cases.
eSign strong

✅ Westpac Ad-Hoc Strengths

  • Third-party document nomination is unique — accountant/broker can submit docs directly

⚠️ Westpac Ad-Hoc Gaps

NAB's mid-application experience is split between NAB Direct and ubank. NAB Direct relies on the customer's banker and online portal for follow-up — similar to Westpac's model. ubank, as a digital-first product, has a more streamlined in-app approach with the 2024 real-time tracker launch. 03 Design Opportunities

01
Unified Tracker — Broker + Direct
No bank offers a seamless identical experience across both channels. A single dashboard regardless of origination is a gap across all four.
02
Proactive ETA Communication
None show "typically 2 days until formal approval." Customers are left guessing — a significant driver of anxiety and inbound calls.
03
Smart Guided Document Capture
CBA has best doc status but no guided camera capture. No bank proactively walks the customer through which document to upload next with live preview.
04
Celebratory Approval Moments
Formal approval and settlement are massive customer life events. No bank creates a memorable, emotionally resonant in-app moment for these milestones.
05
First Home Buyer Guided Journey
FHBs need a step-by-step journey (conveyancer, insurance, council rates, moving). CBA has education content but no guided post-approval checklist experience.
06
Proactive Loan Health Score
Westpac has the visual progress bar. But none offer a "loan health" score or proactive "you could save $X by increasing repayments $Y/mo" recommendation.

What this section covers: Once a customer submits a home loan application, a new set of needs emerges — questions about the process, requests for status updates, ad-hoc information queries ("What documents do I still need?"), and requests to change or add information mid-flight. This section deep-dives into how each of the Big Four banks handles that in-between phase: from application submitted through to formal approval.

The key dimensions examined are: available contact channels, availability hours, who the customer speaks to (AI vs human vs assigned lender), how context is preserved across contacts, and where the biggest CX gaps sit.

The Coach model: ANZ Plus deliberately avoids AI bots as the primary support layer — all queries go to human Coaches, giving a warmer and more personalised experience. The tradeoff is availability: Coaches only operate 9am–5pm AEST on business days, creating a dead zone for evening and weekend queries. The in-app contextual FAQ "Get Help" system partially mitigates this, but there is no ANZ Plus equivalent of Ceba to handle out-of-hours escalation.

07 Key Pain Points by Bank

CommBank — Pain Points
  • Broker-originated applications lose access to full in-app tracker — significant channel inconsistency
  • Pre-approval renewal requires manual initiation — no proactive "your approval expires in 14 days" reminder
  • Joint applicants must both be independently registered in the app — adds setup friction for couples
  • Ceba handles ~60% of queries end-to-end — but complex home loan queries still require specialist and wait times vary
  • No self-serve rate negotiation tool — requires a phone or in-person conversation with a specialist
ANZ Plus — Pain Points
  • Coach support limited to 9am–5pm AEST on business days — no after-hours AI escalation fallback
  • Refinance-only scope (no new purchases, no fixed rate) means many customers can't use ANZ Plus at all
  • Only available in 6 states — TAS and NT customers excluded entirely from ANZ Plus home loan
  • ANZ Plus is siloed from legacy ANZ — existing ANZ Internet Banking customers cannot see ANZ Plus loans
  • Coach response is "not always same day" — asynchronous model requires patience for urgent queries
Westpac — Pain Points
  • Application tracker in a completely separate portal (hlc1.westpac.com.au) — not integrated into Westpac App or Westpac Live
  • Requires Application Number + last name to access tracker — not auto-linked to authenticated session
  • No proactive push notifications during assessment phase — customer must actively check for progress
  • LiveChat (for in-flight queries) is inside the application portal only — not accessible from Westpac App
  • Customer reviews cite long phone hold times (sometimes 1–3hrs) and difficulty reaching assigned lender
  • CDR/Open Banking only available via broker channel — direct customers don't benefit from auto-verification
NAB — Pain Points
  • Split experience between NAB and ubank — different tracker quality, different brand, inconsistent customer journey
  • NAB Direct tracker is web-based and passive — no milestone push notifications for NAB Direct customers
  • 30-day application expiry shorter than Westpac's 60-day window — more pressure on customers in slow property markets
  • Document status less transparent than CommBank — no per-document pending/done status visible in-app
  • No AI virtual assistant for instant query handling — NAB Messaging requires waiting for a human banker to reply
  • ubank real-time tracker still expanding to new purchase customers — initially refinance-focused only

08 Design Opportunities — In-Flight Support

07
AI Triage with Human Warm Handoff
ANZ Plus's human-first model is warm but limited in hours. CommBank's AI is available 24/7 but can feel cold. The opportunity is an AI layer that handles FAQs at any hour and seamlessly escalates to a named coach with full context — not a generic agent pool.
08
Proactive Application ETA
None of the Big 4 tell customers "your application is typically approved within 2 business days from this point." Showing a real-time ETA bar ("We're working on it — typically done by Thursday") would dramatically reduce status-query calls and anxiety.
09
Application Support Unified Across Channels
Westpac's application portal LiveChat is completely disconnected from the Westpac App. The ideal state is a single thread of communication — whether initiated in the app, the portal or via phone — visible in one place, with full history.
10
Proactive Pre-Approval Expiry Reminders
CommBank's conditional pre-approval doesn't send a proactive "expires in 14 days" push notification. All four banks miss this moment — a high-anxiety touchpoint for customers still searching for property that is easy to design proactively.
11
Contextual Document Request Explanations
When a bank requests additional documents mid-assessment, customers often don't understand why. An in-app explanation ("We need your last 3 payslips because X") with a direct upload link would reduce friction and improve document quality — reducing re-requests.
12
Broker + Direct Parity
CommBank tracker access is limited for broker-originated applications. Westpac's broker CDR advantage doesn't pass through to direct customers. There is a clear opportunity to build a tracker experience that works identically regardless of origination channel.
